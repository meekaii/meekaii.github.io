BENTOTA HATCHERY

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a popular attraction in Bentota for all travellers. The pristine golden shores that draws people to its island, also provides an enticing nesting ground for several endangered turtle species from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The project aims at conserving and protecting the eggs from predators and other dangers.



Things To Do


Guided Tours: Take a guided tour of the hatchery to learn about the different species of sea turtles, their life cycles, nesting habits, and conservation efforts. Knowledgeable staff members will provide insights into the hatchery's activities and answer any questions you may have.

Observe Sea Turtles: Get up close and observe sea turtles of various sizes and species. The hatchery will have tanks and pools where you can see different stages of sea turtles, from tiny hatchlings to adult turtles undergoing rehabilitation.

Hatchling Releases: If you're fortunate, you might have the opportunity to participate in or witness a hatchling release. This incredible experience allows you to release baby turtles into the ocean, contributing to their survival and promoting conservation awareness. (This event takes place at sunset.)


Conservative Efforts At Bentota Hatchery

Nesting Site Protection: The hatchery identifies and protects sea turtle nesting sites along the coastal areas of Bentota. By monitoring these sites and implementing measures to safeguard the nests from disturbances and threats, the hatchery ensures a safe and conducive environment for nesting turtles.

Incubation and Release: One of the primary activities at the hatchery is the collection of sea turtle eggs from nesting sites. These eggs are carefully incubated in controlled environments to maximize their hatching success. Once the hatchlings emerge, the hatchery organizes regular releases, allowing visitors to witness and participate in the incredible journey of the baby turtles as they make their way into the ocean.

Rescue and Rehabilitation: The hatchery provides a sanctuary for injured or sick sea turtles. Skilled staff members and veterinarians offer medical treatment and rehabilitation to these turtles, aiming to restore their health and eventually release them back into the wild.

Education and Awareness Initiatives

Visitor Education: The hatchery offers guided tours and informative sessions to educate visitors about the biology, behavior, and conservation status of sea turtles. Visitors have the opportunity to learn about the threats faced by sea turtles and the importance of their protection, inspiring them to become advocates for conservation.

School Programs: The hatchery actively engages with local schools and educational institutions, conducting interactive programs and workshops to educate students about sea turtle conservation. These programs aim to instill a sense of environmental responsibility and encourage young minds to actively participate in protecting sea turtles and their habitats.

Community Involvement and Sustainable Practices

Community Outreach: The hatchery collaborates with local communities, fishermen, and stakeholders to raise awareness and promote sustainable practices. Through workshops, training sessions, and awareness campaigns, the hatchery fosters a sense of responsibility and encourages community members to actively participate in turtle conservation efforts.

Sustainable Fishing Practices: The hatchery advocates for sustainable fishing practices among local fishermen, aiming to reduce accidental turtle bycatch and promote responsible fishing methods. By working closely with the fishing community, the hatchery helps minimize the impact on sea turtles and their habitats.

Environmental Conservation: The hatchery actively supports environmental conservation initiatives, such as beach cleanup campaigns and mangrove restoration projects. These efforts contribute to maintaining clean and healthy ecosystems, which are essential for the well-being and survival of sea turtles.
